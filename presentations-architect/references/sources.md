# Key Sources and Authorities

Named references cited throughout this skill. When making recommendations, cite the relevant authority — "Abela's chart chooser recommends..." is stronger than "use a bar chart."

---

## Essential books by function

**Executive presentation structure**: *The Pyramid Principle* by Barbara Minto (1987/2020) — answer-first, logically structured communication. Foundational to McKinsey, BCG, all major consulting firms.

**Data storytelling process**: *Storytelling with Data* by Cole Nussbaumer Knaflic (2015) — 6-step process: understand context → choose visual → eliminate clutter → draw attention → think like designer → tell story. Used at 100+ universities.

**Narrative design**: *Resonate* by Nancy Duarte (2010) — the Sparkline. Taught at Stanford GSB. *Slide:ology* (2008) covers visual thinking. *DataStory* (2019) bridges visualization and narrative.

**Data storytelling theory**: *Effective Data Storytelling* by Brent Dykes (2020) — Data + Narrative + Visuals as intersecting pillars.

**Visualization theory**: *The Visual Display of Quantitative Information* by Edward Tufte (1983/2001) — data-ink ratio, chartjunk, Lie Factor, small multiples, sparklines. Amazon top 100 non-fiction of the 20th century.

**Practical chart design**: *Show Me the Numbers* by Stephen Few (2012); *Good Charts* by Scott Berinato (2016, HBR Press); *Fundamentals of Data Visualization* by Claus Wilke (2019, also free online).

**Critical visual literacy**: *How Charts Lie* by Alberto Cairo (2019); *The Truthful Art* (2016).

**Memorable communication**: *Made to Stick* by Chip and Dan Heath (2007) — SUCCESs framework.

## Key frameworks

**FT Visual Vocabulary**: 72 chart types in 9 categories. Most widely adopted professional chart selection reference. ft-interactive.github.io/visual-vocabulary/

**The Graphic Continuum** (Schwabish & Ribecca): ~90 chart types across 6 categories with cross-category connections.

**Abela's Chart Chooser**: Original 4-category decision tree. Simple, fast, widely known.

**Evergreen's Chart Choosers**: Plain-language goal-based selection. Only qualitative data visualization framework.

**From Data to Viz**: Data-format-first interactive decision tree at data-to-viz.com with caveat gallery.

## Foundational academic research

**Cleveland & McGill (1984)**, "Graphical Perception," *JASA* — ranking perceptual accuracy of visual encodings: Position > Length > Angle > Area > Color saturation > Hue. Underlies all modern chart selection.

**Heer & Bostock (2010)**, "Crowdsourcing Graphical Perception," *CHI* — replicated Cleveland & McGill via Mechanical Turk. Bostock created D3.js.

**Bertin (1967/1983)**, *Semiology of Graphics* — original theory of visual variables (position, size, shape, value, color, orientation, texture).

**Mackinlay (1986)**, "Automating the Design of Graphical Presentations" — extended Cleveland & McGill into automated systems, influenced Tableau.

## Key practitioners

**Edward Tufte**: Yale professor emeritus. Coined data-ink ratio, chartjunk. Four foundational books.

**Cole Nussbaumer Knaflic**: Former Google analytics. Five books. Made data visualization accessible at scale.

**Nancy Duarte**: CEO of largest Silicon Valley design firm. Created presentations for Apple and Al Gore. Six books.

**Barbara Minto**: First female MBA hire at McKinsey. Invented Pyramid Principle and MECE.

**Stephen Few**: Founder of Perceptual Edge. Six books on evidence-based data presentation.

**Alberto Cairo**: Knight Chair in Visual Journalism, University of Miami. Visual literacy and ethics.

**Scott Berinato**: HBR Senior Editor. The 2×2 typology for data visualization.

**Brent Dykes**: Leading authority on data storytelling. DAA "Most Influential" 2016.

**Jon Schwabish**: Urban Institute. Created The Graphic Continuum and PolicyViz.

**Andy Kirk**: Visualisingdata.com. Clients include Google, CERN, McKinsey.
